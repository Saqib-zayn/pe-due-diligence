INVESTMENT MEMORANDUM
NovaCast Industrial Limited
Prepared by: Meridian Capital Partners
Date: March 2026
Classification: Strictly Confidential

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NovaCast Industrial Limited ("NovaCast" or "the Company") is a mid-market UK manufacturer specialising in precision investment casting components for the aerospace and automotive sectors. Founded in 2009 and headquartered in Sheffield, South Yorkshire, the Company operates two production facilities totalling 84,000 square feet and employs a workforce of 312 full-time personnel. NovaCast has established long-term supply relationships with Tier 1 aerospace contractors including Rolls-Royce, GKN Aerospace, and several European automotive OEMs.

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE

For the fiscal year ended December 2025, NovaCast reported revenues of £47.3 million, representing a year-on-year revenue growth of 18.4% against the prior period. The Company has demonstrated consistent top-line expansion over a five-year horizon, compounding at approximately 14% annually since 2020. EBITDA for FY2025 reached £9.2 million, yielding an EBITDA margin of 19.5%, underpinned by ongoing operational efficiency improvements and favourable long-term supply contracts with fixed pricing mechanisms.

The Company's balance sheet reflects total financial debt of £11.8 million against shareholder equity of £22.4 million, resulting in a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.53. This moderate leverage profile provides significant headroom for acquisition financing or facility expansion investment without materially elevating financial risk. Capital expenditure for FY2025 was £3.1 million, directed primarily toward CNC machining upgrades and quality inspection automation.

MARKET POSITION AND GROWTH DRIVERS

The global investment casting market is valued at approximately £18.6 billion and is forecast to expand at a 6.8% CAGR through 2031, driven by increasing demand for lightweight, high-tolerance components in next-generation aerospace platforms and electric vehicle powertrains. NovaCast occupies a defensible niche position supplying technically complex aluminium and titanium castings that require AS9100D aerospace certification, creating meaningful barriers to entry for potential competitors.

The Company's order backlog stands at £31.2 million as of Q4 2025, representing approximately 8 months of forward revenue visibility. Management has identified expansion opportunities into the defence sector following recent MoD framework procurement initiatives.

RISK CONSIDERATIONS

Supply chain concentration in titanium feedstock sourced predominantly from two suppliers introduces input cost volatility. Macro-sensitivity to aerospace OEM production cycles creates revenue lumpiness risk. Integration of acquired competitor facilities may present short-term margin dilution. Skilled labour attrition within specialist casting operations remains an ongoing management challenge given regional competition for qualified metallurgical engineers.

INVESTMENT THESIS

NovaCast presents a compelling investment opportunity within the UK advanced manufacturing landscape. The combination of demonstrated revenue growth, healthy margins, conservative leverage, substantial forward order visibility, and a defensible technical market position supports a Strong Buy assessment by this advisory team.
